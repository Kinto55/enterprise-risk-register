## Project Overview
A risk register with no issue tracking or SLA enforcement is just administrative noise. This project bridges the gap between theoretical compliance and production reality. I built an end-to-end Risk and Controls Management Architecture for a simulated model enterprise SaaS architecture. This project demonstrates how to transition from simply identifying raw technical vulnerabilities to actively auditing control failures, evaluating operational effectiveness, and enforcing rigorous enterprise risk governance.

## Core Components
This repository contains the core pillars of a functional, production-ready GRC architecture:
1. **Quantitative Risk Register:** A comprehensive risk matrix scoring 11 critical SaaS vulnerabilities using a 1–25 Impact x Likelihood methodology, defining inherent risks, risk categories, and structured mitigation plans.
2. **Control Effectiveness Issue Tracker & Audit:** A granular audit of 20 simulated operational issues, identifying where technical and administrative controls (such as MFA, RBAC, access reviews, and vendor offboarding) failed in the real world, resulting in upwardly adjusted residual risk scores.
3. **Control Remediation & SLA Enforcement Framework:** A system tying operational findings directly to structured remediation lifecycles, clear departmental ownership, and executive escalation paths.
4. **Executive Risk Dashboard:** A visual translation of raw risk data into a Risk Heatmap and categorical dashboard designed for C-suite visibility and strategic decision-making.

## Key Highlights & Governance Mechanics
* **Dynamic Risk Scoring:** Rather than accepting static "paper compliance," residual risk scores were actively recalibrated upward based on real control failures (e.g., escalating Risk-001 to a critical residual score of 25 due to active MFA bypasses).
* **Strict SLA Enforcement:** Tied critical vulnerabilities directly to a maximum 15-day remediation window (with a 7-day target) to enforce accountability across business units. 
* **Cross-Functional Ownership:** Mapped each risk to specific departmental owners (Engineering, IT, HR, Legal, etc.) to ensure remediation tasks had designated business accountability.
